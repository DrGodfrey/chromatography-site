How much sample are you planning to load onto your column in grams?

Generally you should use somewhere in the range 10–50 times as much silica as you have sample (by weight). 20–25× should be enough for most seperations, but more can offer marginal benefit for more challenging seperations (up to about 50×). As a rough rule of thumb, you need 4× as much silica to get 2× as good seperation, so the benefits of excessive silica fall off rapidly.

Rf for the target compound in your column. Generally an Rf in the range 0.20–0.35 is recommended. Rfs on the lower end of the range are likely to allow better seperation, but require more solvent and will lead to more diluted fractions - which sometimes makes identifying your products & impurities more challenging. Although ideally avoided, for trickier seperations very low Rfs (0.15-0.2) are probably a better option than excessive silica usage.

I've seen seen a range of estimates for the density of silica on the order of 0.3–0.5 g/mL, so 0.4 g/mL seems like a fairly safe placeholder until I get more feedback. The exact silica density shouldn't have a substantial effect on the outcome of your column (especially if you choose to measure the silica by volume), I've nonetheless included an option to adjust this for anyone who knows the specific value for the silica they're working with.
